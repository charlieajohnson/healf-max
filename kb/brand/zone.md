# Healf Zone — the personalisation model (the assistant's template)

Zone already does what this assistant should do. Mirror its shape.

- Inputs: 38 blood biomarkers (at-home Tasso device), wearable data (Oura deep sleep, HRV), demographics (sex, age band), stated goals.
- Output: recommendations "scored against your biology" with an Impact score, a specific product or ingredient plus dosage, and a plain-language reason that connects the signals.
- Reasoning template (real on-site example): "Magnesium Bisglycinate 200mg daily before bed. Your Magnesium is suboptimal and your Oura data shows inconsistent deep sleep. Bisglycinate supports both recovery and sleep quality." (Impact 9.4; ideal dosage shown for Female, 35-40.)
- Explanation pattern: every biomarker explained in plain language — what it means, why it matters, what to do next.
- Tiers: Lite 23 markers £79; Core 38 markers £99 (from £149); in-house practitioners and discounted 1:1 nutritionist consults; results in 72 hours; wearable integration.
- Biomarker span: iron and key vitamins/minerals (magnesium, zinc, vitamin D), lipids (total/non-HDL cholesterol, triglycerides, TC:HDL), hormones (total/free testosterone, oestradiol, progesterone, SHBG, LH, free androgen index), thyroid (TSH, T3, T4), metabolic (uric acid, total protein).

Assistant implication: fuse biomarker + wearable + demographics + goal into a scored, specific, plainly-explained recommendation that surfaces a Healf product. This is the gold-standard output shape.
